# STRATEGY.md — Outreach Strategy & Messaging Playbook
> Last updated: 2026-03-22

## Outreach Philosophy
Holigenix does not sell. Holigenix positions itself as a **clinical resource** for families and discharge teams. Every outreach should communicate:
1. We specialize in the most complex pediatric cases (vent/trach/g-tube)
2. We can staff quickly and reliably
3. We want to be a resource for your families
4. We make your job easier (smooth transitions, responsive communication)

## Messaging Frameworks

### Introductory Email (Cold Contact)
**When**: First time reaching out to a new contact
**Tone**: Clinical credibility + resource framing
**Structure**:
1. Brief intro — who we are (1 sentence)
2. What we do — specialize in medically fragile pediatric home nursing (1-2 sentences)
3. Why we're reaching out — want to be a resource for their team and families (1 sentence)
4. Soft ask — offer an in-service, lunch-and-learn, or introductory call (1 sentence)
5. Professional sign-off

**Subject line patterns that work**:
- "Pediatric Home Nursing Resource for [Hospital Name] Families"
- "Supporting [Department] Discharge Planning — Holigenix Healthcare"
- "Skilled Nursing for Medically Fragile Children — Partnership Inquiry"

### Follow-up Email
**When**: After initial outreach, maintaining cadence
**Tone**: Value-add + gentle persistence
**Structure**:
1. Reference last interaction or outreach
2. Provide value (staffing update, new capability, case study without PHI)
3. Reiterate availability
4. Specific next step suggestion

### Meeting Request
**When**: Relationship is warm enough for a face-to-face
**Tone**: Specific and easy to say yes to
**Structure**:
1. Context — reference existing relationship or mutual connection
2. Purpose — specific reason for meeting (not generic "catch up")
3. Format — lunch-and-learn, in-service for their team, coffee meeting
4. Flexibility — offer 2-3 time options

### Re-engagement
**When**: Contact has gone cold (30+ days no response)
**Tone**: Acknowledge gap + lead with value
**Structure**:
1. Acknowledge it's been a while (1 sentence, no guilt)
2. Share something new (staffing expansion, new capability, seasonal relevance)
3. Soft re-open (not pushy)

### Thank You
**When**: After receiving a referral or having a meeting
**Tone**: Genuine gratitude + reinforce partnership
**Structure**:
1. Specific thanks (name what you're thanking them for)
2. Brief update on intake status (no PHI — just "we're moving forward")
3. Reinforce the partnership value

## What Doesn't Work (Update as you learn)
- Generic "just checking in" messages — too vague
- Sales-heavy language — turns off clinical professionals
- Lengthy emails — keep under 200 words
- Reaching out without knowing the contact's role — do research first

## Outreach Cadence
| Contact Stage | Follow-up Frequency |
|--------------|-------------------|
| New (never contacted) | Intro email, then follow up in 5-7 days |
| Contacted (no response) | Follow up every 10-14 days, max 3 attempts |
| Engaged (responded) | Follow up every 7-10 days to nurture |
| Active Partner (referring) | Touch base every 2-3 weeks, thank after every referral |
| Cold (was active, went quiet) | Re-engagement at 30 days, then monthly |

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize
1. **Vent/trach/g-tube expertise** — not every agency can handle this level of complexity
2. **Speed** — fast intake, quick PA processing, rapid staffing
3. **Reliability** — we staff what we commit to
4. **Communication** — responsive, easy to reach, proactive updates
5. **GAPP specialization** — we know the program inside and out
