# GAPP_KNOWLEDGE.md — Georgia Pediatric Program & Referral Intelligence
> Last updated: 2026-03-22

## GAPP Program Overview
- **Full name**: Georgia Pediatric Program (GAPP)
- **Type**: Medicaid waiver program for medically fragile children under 21 in Georgia
- **Administered by**: Georgia Department of Community Health (DCH) via Medicaid
- **Services covered**: Private Duty Nursing (PDN), Skilled Nursing Visits
- **Eligibility**: Children who are technology-dependent, medically complex, or require ongoing nursing supervision

## Eligible Patient Characteristics
- Ventilator-dependent
- Tracheostomy requiring suctioning/monitoring
- Gastrostomy tube (g-tube) feeding
- Central line management
- Oxygen-dependent
- Seizure disorders requiring monitoring
- Complex medication regimens
- Post-NICU discharge with ongoing nursing needs

## Authorization Process
1. **Referral received** from hospital/MCO/physician
2. **Insurance verification** — confirm Medicaid/MCO coverage
3. **Prior Authorization (PA)** — submit to MCO for approval
4. **Level of Need (LON) assessment** — clinical evaluation determining hours approved
5. **Staffing** — assign qualified nurse(s)
6. **Start of Care** — begin home nursing services
- **Key KPI**: Time from referral receipt to Start of Care (goal: minimize)

## LON (Level of Need) Scoring
- Higher LON score = more nursing hours approved
- Based on clinical acuity, technology dependence, caregiver availability
- Reassessed periodically by MCO
- Agencies should document thoroughly to support maximum appropriate hours

## Referral Source Types (Priority Order)
1. **NICU Social Workers** — highest value; handle discharge planning for medically fragile newborns. These are the #1 target.
2. **PICU Social Workers** — step-down for critically ill children
3. **Pediatric Case Managers** — coordinate post-discharge care for complex patients
4. **Hospital Discharge Planners** — manage transition plans for all discharging patients
5. **MCO Care Coordinators** — assign care coordinators to complex Medicaid members; can refer directly
6. **Pediatric Specialist Physicians** — neurologists, pulmonologists, cardiologists, intensivists
7. **Early Intervention / CPSE Coordinators** — know families with medically fragile children
8. **Pediatric Rehab Discharge Coordinators** — PT/OT/ST teams in hospital settings

## What Referral Sources Care About (in order)
1. **Can you staff the case?** — Staffing capacity is the #1 concern. If you can't staff, they stop calling.
2. **How fast can you start?** — Discharge timelines are tight. Speed wins.
3. **Do you handle complex kids?** — Vent/trach/g-tube competency is a credibility signal.
4. **Are you easy to work with?** — Communication, responsiveness, follow-through matter.
5. **Do you accept their insurance?** — Medicaid/MCO coverage verification.

## How Referrals Work (End-to-End)
1. Hospital social worker or discharge planner identifies a child needing home nursing
2. They contact home health agencies to request intake
3. Agency verifies Medicaid/MCO eligibility
4. Agency initiates PA process with MCO
5. Agency clinician conducts LON assessment
6. Once PA approved, agency staffs and schedules
7. **The relationship with the referral source drives future referrals** — agencies with strong relationships get called first

## NICU Approach Strategy
- Lead with clinical credibility — mention vent/trach/g-tube competency
- Emphasize responsiveness and intake speed
- Offer in-service or lunch-and-learn for the NICU team
- Frame as "we want to be a resource for your families" — never cold-sell
- Follow up consistently — NICU social workers change frequently; stay top of mind
- Bring clinical materials (brochures, capability sheets) to leave behind

## Referral Pipeline Stages
```
New Referral → Insurance Verification → Staffing → Start of Care → Converted
```
**Common bottlenecks**: Slow insurance verification, staffing shortages, LON assessment delays, MCO PA turnaround time

## Compliance Notes
- Never reference PHI (patient names, diagnoses, treatment details) in any outreach
- Never claim access to hospital patient data or discharge lists
- Only use publicly available hospital information and CRM-entered contact data
- All outreach must be professional and relationship-focused, not sales-focused
